# Lead-Gen Principles for Local Social Content

Load this when writing the 5 posts. These are the rules that separate "content that gets likes"
from "content that books appointments."

## What the algorithm AND your pipeline both reward
- **Saves, shares, and DM sends** outrank likes. They signal value and drive distribution.
- **DM sends are the single strongest Reel ranking signal** — so every post should engineer a DM
  (a comment keyword you reply to, or a "DM me X").

## Format priority (highest lead intent first)
1. **Reels** — ~2× the reach of static posts, highest engagement (~3.7%) in most local niches.
   Post ~3/week. Sweet spot 30–90s.
2. **Carousels** — best for education/trust ("3 things before you…", myth-busting).
3. **Stories** — nurture warm leads, not cold reach.

## The 5 content pillars that convert locally
- **Buyer/Customer-intent** — "What $X gets you in [City]", price-anchored. Surfaces ready buyers.
- **Seller/Provider education** — pricing, market myths. Builds trust with the higher-commission side.
- **Relocation/Newcomer** — "POV: you just moved to [City]". Reaches out-of-market, high-value clients.
- **Hyper-local authority** — neighborhood insider picks, "areas heating up". Heavy saves = expertise.
- **Actionable education** — "3 things to do before…". The most-shared format; answers a real question.

## Hook rules (first 3 seconds decide everything)
- Text overlay on screen — ~half watch with no sound.
- Answer a burning question ("How much house can you buy for $500K in [City]?").
- Or use POV / curiosity gap ("Sellers don't want you to know you can ask for this right now…").
- Always include the **city name** for local SEO.

## CTA rules
- One clear action per post: a keyword to **comment** or **DM** (TOUR, PRICE, MAP…).
- Say it out loud AND put it in the caption.
- Reply to every comment with a DM — triggers the conversation and the algorithm.

## Tone guardrail
The agent does research + strategy; the human adds taste and voice. Never produce generic "AI slop."
Every post must be specific to THIS market and THIS week's data.
